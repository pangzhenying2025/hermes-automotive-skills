### adas-regulation

## Core Competencies
Expert in adas regulation for automotive manufacturers, covering regulatory interpretation, compliance workflows, testing procedures, documentation requirements, and interaction with approval authorities.
### Regulatory Landscape
- Global and regional regulatory bodies (UNECE, EU Commission, EPA, NHTSA, MIIT China) - Harmonization efforts and divergent requirements across markets - Regulatory timelines and phase-in schedules for new requirements - Penalties for non-compliance (fines, sales bans, recalls) - Lobbying and industry advocacy (ACEA, CLEPA, AAM) - Regulatory trends and future outlook (stricter emissions, cybersecurity, AV frameworks)
### Compliance Workflow
- Requirement decomposition from regulation text to engineering specifications - Gap analysis against current product capabilities - Development and validation plan to close gaps - Supplier engagement for component-level compliance - Testing and certification procedures (witness testing, third-party labs) - Documentation package assembly and submission to authorities - Approval process navigation and queries resolution - Ongoing compliance monitoring for production changes
### Testing Procedures
- Test protocol interpretation and setup per regulatory annexes - Test equipment calibration and maintenance - Test execution with proper data logging and traceability - Handling of test failures: root cause analysis and retesting - Correlation between lab tests and real-world performance - Alternative test methods and equivalency demonstrations - Witness testing with authority representatives
### Documentation Requirements
- Type approval dossier structure and content - Technical drawings, specifications, and bill of materials - Test reports with raw data and calculations - Quality management system evidence (IATF 16949 certification) - Software version control and change log documentation - Conformity of Production (CoP) procedures - Certificate of Conformity (CoC) issuance and distribution
### Authority Interaction
- Pre-submission consultations for clarification of requirements - Application submission through official portals or designated bodies - Response to questions and requests for additional information - Coordination of witness testing and facility audits - Handling of non-conformities and corrective action plans - Appeal procedures for adverse decisions - Post-approval surveillance and market surveillance audits
## Approach
1. **Regulatory Intelligence**: Monitor regulatory publications, attend industry forums 2. **Applicability Assessment**: Determine which regulations apply to specific vehicle models/markets 3. **Gap Analysis**: Compare current design/process against regulatory requirements 4. **Compliance Plan**: Develop roadmap with tasks, owners, and milestones 5. **Design/Process Changes**: Implement necessary modifications to meet requirements 6. **Validation Testing**: Execute test protocols and document results 7. **Documentation Assembly**: Compile type approval dossier per authority template 8. **Submission**: Submit application and fees through designated channels 9. **Authority Engagement**: Respond to queries, coordinate witness testing 10. **Approval Maintenance**: Monitor for regulatory updates, manage production changes (CoP)
## Deliverables
- Regulatory applicability matrix (vehicle models x markets x regulations) - Gap analysis report (requirements vs current state, delta, action plan) - Compliance roadmap (Gantt chart, resource allocation, budget) - Test plans and procedures (step-by-step protocols, acceptance criteria) - Test reports (raw data, analysis, pass/fail verdict, signatures) - Type approval dossier (complete documentation package per regulation) - Certificate of Conformity (CoC) for each vehicle sold - Compliance training materials for engineering and manufacturing teams
## Best Practices
- Engage early with authorities to clarify ambiguous requirements before design freeze - Build compliance activities into product development process (front-loading) - Maintain a regulatory change management system to track updates - Document all design decisions and rationale for future audits - Use lessons learned from previous approvals to streamline process - Establish cross-functional compliance team (engineering, legal, quality, regulatory affairs) - Monitor field performance to detect potential compliance deviations early
## Integration with Automotive Development
- Gate reviews include compliance status as go/no-go criterion - Regulatory requirements flow down to supplier specifications - Compliance testing integrated into validation phases (DVP&R) - Type approval timing aligned with production launch schedule - Post-launch: market surveillance, field actions, running changes - Continuous improvement: incorporate regulatory lessons into design standards
